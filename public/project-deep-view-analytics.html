Deep View Analytics

Data Platform | Connected Vehicle Intelligence

Business Problem

A connected vehicle platform needed real-time analytics across over a million daily OBD-II telemetry events for fleet management, driver behavior analysis, and predictive maintenance. The legacy batch processing system could not support operational decisions that required sub-minute latency, and infrastructure costs were growing faster than revenue.

Strategic Decision

We designed a real-time streaming architecture with cost-conscious infrastructure from day one. Rather than migrating the batch system incrementally, we built a parallel platform with full observability instrumented into every pipeline stage, allowing us to right-size infrastructure continuously and avoid the cost overruns that plagued the previous system.

Architecture at a Glance

PySpark for large-scale telemetry processing, FastAPI for service APIs, PostgreSQL and MongoDB for structured and unstructured storage, RabbitMQ for message queuing, and Docker/Kubernetes for orchestration. Full CI/CD pipeline with observability (SLIs, SLOs, tracing, cost attribution) across every processing stage.

Key Trade-offs

  • Open-source stack over vendor lock-in: Higher operational overhead but long-term portability and cost control.
  • Observability as a first-class feature: Upfront investment in instrumentation enabled the 30% cost reduction that justified the entire platform rebuild.
  • Parallel build over incremental migration: Higher short-term cost, but clean architecture unencumbered by legacy constraints.

Business Outcome

Over 1 million telemetry events processed daily with sub-minute latency. 30% infrastructure cost reduction ($36k annual savings) achieved through observability-driven optimization. The platform supports fleet management, driver behavior analytics, carbon tracking, and predictive maintenance on a single shared backbone.
