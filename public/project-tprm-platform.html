Key Components

Request Orchestration: Manages incoming requests, routing, and workflow control

Agent Runtime: Dynamically constructs AI agents for context-driven execution

Document Intelligence: Extracts and classifies structured and unstructured risk data

State Management (Cosmos DB): Tracks workflow stages and ensures persistence

Messaging Layer (Service Bus): Enables asynchronous and scalable communication

Integration Layer (ServiceNow): Sends processed results back to enterprise systems

Architecture Decisions

  • Event-driven architecture to enable scalability, decoupling, and asynchronous processing of risk workflows
  • Agent-based execution model to dynamically construct AI workflows based on request context instead of static pipelines
  • Asynchronous messaging (Azure Service Bus) to ensure resilience, fault tolerance, and high throughput
  • Cosmos DB for state management to maintain workflow state, enable traceability, and support distributed execution

AI-Driven TPRM Platform

Risk & Compliance | Global Consulting Firm

Business Problem

A global consulting firm needed to evaluate third-party vendor risk across thousands of suppliers. The existing process was manual, took weeks per vendor, was error-prone, and could not scale to meet regulatory and compliance deadlines. The business needed a way to automate risk assessment without sacrificing accuracy or auditability.

Strategic Decision

Rather than augmenting the manual review process with tooling, we chose to build an event-driven AI platform from scratch. The key bet was that an agent-based architecture with document intelligence could handle the full assessment lifecycle end-to-end, from intake through risk scoring to workflow delivery, without human intervention for routine cases.

Architecture at a Glance

Cloud-native on Azure with ServiceNow as the integration layer. Azure Service Bus handles asynchronous communication between services. An Agent Runtime orchestrates context-driven AI workflows dynamically. Document Intelligence extracts and classifies structured and unstructured risk data. Cosmos DB provides workflow state, traceability, and distributed execution support.

Key Trade-offs

  • Event-driven over synchronous: Higher initial complexity, but fault tolerance and independent scaling for each processing stage.
  • Cosmos DB over relational: Schema flexibility for diverse vendor document structures; eventual consistency acceptable for this domain.
  • Agent Runtime over static pipelines: Dynamic workflow construction enables new risk assessment types without code changes.

Business Outcome

Reduced vendor risk assessment cycle from weeks of manual review to minutes of automated processing. The platform scales horizontally to handle enterprise-level vendor volumes and provides full audit trails for compliance. Routine risk assessments now require zero manual intervention.
